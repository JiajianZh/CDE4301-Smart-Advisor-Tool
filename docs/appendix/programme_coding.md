## RIASEC Coding Schema

Each NUS undergraduate programme receives:
- **Primary RIASEC code** (mandatory): The dominant activity type
- **Secondary RIASEC code** (mandatory): A significant component (20-40%)
- **Tertiary RIASEC code** (optional): For multifaceted programmes
- **Value tags** (3-5 per programme): Keywords like "innovation," "helping-people," "high-salary"

## Coding Rationale Examples

### Why is Biomedical Engineering coded I-R-S?

- **Primary I (Investigative):** Most modules focus on research, analysis, and understanding biological systems
- **Secondary R (Realistic):** Significant hands-on lab work, prototyping medical devices
- **Tertiary S (Social):** Applications directly help people through healthcare technology
- **Value tags:** "helping-people" because career outcomes involve improving patient care; "technology" and "innovation" because of medical device development

### Why is Computer Science coded I-C?

- **Primary I (Investigative):** Core activities involve problem-solving, algorithm design, theoretical understanding
- **Secondary C (Conventional):** Significant amount of systematic coding, following syntax rules, debugging, data structures
- **Not R:** While CS involves building things (programs), the activity is more intellectual/logical than hands-on/mechanical
- **Value tags:** "technology" and "innovation" are obvious; "high-salary" reflects strong graduate employment outcomes

### Why is Psychology coded I-S?

- **Primary I (Investigative):** Programme emphasizes research methods, statistical analysis, understanding mental processes
- **Secondary S (Social):** Focus on understanding human behavior, counseling skills, interpersonal dynamics
- **Not S-I:** Despite working with people, the academic programme is more research-heavy than directly helping people
- **Value tags:** "understanding-behavior" is central; "research" reflects methodology emphasis; "helping-people" for clinical/counseling applications
